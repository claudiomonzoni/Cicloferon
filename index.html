What are cold sores

Cold sores —also called fever blisters— are small, fluid-filled blisters that form on or around the lips, sometimes grouped together in patches.

They’re usually caused by herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1). It’s important to remember that cold sores are very common, as they can spread easily, and there’s usually no need to panic.

    What are the symptoms?

  • Unsightly small, fluid-filled blisters

  • An annoying itching, burning, or tingling sensationon or around the lips

  • Blisters that ooze fluid and then crust over

WHAT CAUSES COLD SORES?

  • Fever
  • Stress
  • Viral infections
  • Wekened immune system
  • Fatigue
  • Hormonal changes
  • Dry and cold wheather
  • Exessive sun exposure
It’s very common! 46% of adults worldwide have a history of cold sores

What can I do to prevent them?

  • Avoid kissing others, as it can spread easily through body fluids and close contact

  • Avoid sharing drinks, utensils, or cups with others, especially people you don’t know

  • Practice some stress-reducing habits, such as getting regular exercise and enough sleep

  • Protect your lips from the sun by wearing hats or sunscreen

  • Boost your immune system by eating well-balanced meals and minimizing alcohol & caffeine in your diet
